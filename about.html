About Audi

Audi is a German automotive manufacturer known for its luxury vehicles, performance, and innovation.


History

The Volkswagen Group acquired Auto Union from Daimler-Benz in 1964, and NSU Motorenwerke in 1969. The first modern Audi-branded vehicles were released in 1968.

Ownership

In 2020, Volkswagen AG announced that it would take over all Audi shares it didn't own, making Audi a fully owned subsidiary of the Volkswagen Group.

Other brands

The Volkswagen Group also owns other high-performance automakers, including Lamborghini, Bugatti, Porsche, and Bentley. Audi is a premium car manufacturer that designs, develops, manufactures, and sells cars and motorcycles globally. The company is headquartered in Ingolstadt, Bayern, Germany.

Audi is a subsidiary of the Volkswagen Group. This has allowed Audi to continue to create high-quality performance vehicles appointed with some of the most sophisticated features on the market.
